Manifesto

No Gods, No Masters, No Galleries

A declaration of creative and spiritual liberation

ğŸ”¥ Who We Are

In a world carved up by capitalists, bordered by nation-states, and controlled by those who profit from our labor, we choose autonomy. We are artists, healers, spiritual practitioners, nomads, and refuseniks who will not be catalogued, commodified, or contained.

ENAMTILA.space is not an organization â€” it is organized chaos, a temporary autonomous zone, a living contradiction. Named after the Sumerian "House of Life," we reclaim a word older than every empire that tried to silence us.

We believe that art, healing, and revolution are inseparable. Creativity without resistance is decoration for the ruling class. Resistance without creativity is joyless duty. We fuse both: our existence is aesthetic rebellion, our rebellion is artistic and spiritual practice.

ğŸš« What We Reject

We reject borders that separate practitioners based on where they were born. Geography is an accident of birth, not a definition of identity or destiny.

We reject gatekeepers who determine whose art or spiritual practice is "legitimate" and whose is not. The gallery owners, critics, religious institutions, and "wellness" corporations who profit from controlling access have no power over us.

We reject exploitation of creators and healers by those who would commodify creativity and spirituality for profit while leaving practitioners struggling to survive. Art and healing are not products; they are manifestations of the human spirit.

We reject hierarchy that elevates some practices over others. Whether you paint, heal, write, dance, coach, or create in ways yet unnamed â€” your practice is valid, valuable, and vital.

We reject isolation that keeps practitioners separated and struggling alone. Together we are powerful. Together we are unstoppable. Together we are ENAMTILA.

âš¡ What We Sabotage

We sabotage capitalism that turns art and healing into commodity and practitioners into content creators. No more unpaid internships. No more "passion projects" that exploit us.

We sabotage nation-states that criminalize movement, cage migrants, and murder people for crossing imaginary lines. Your papers mean nothing. Your borders are violence. We cross them anyway.

We sabotage art institutions â€” the museums built on colonial plunder, the galleries that launder oligarch money, the wellness industry that gentrifies spiritual practices. We will never ask permission to create or heal.

We sabotage "professional" art and spirituality that require degrees, certifications, connections, and wealth to access. Art school is a class barrier. Institutional spirituality is control. We learn from each other, in squats and streets, not classrooms.

ğŸ”® Our Vision

We envision a world where every practitioner has a place to create and heal, where housing is abundant and shared freely among our global family, where no creative or healing soul ever sleeps on the street while empty apartments stand as monuments to greed.

We envision a world where art and healing are accessible to all, where galleries and healing spaces exist in streets and parks, where performances and ceremonies happen in squares and marketplaces, where creativity and spirituality are woven into the fabric of daily life.

We envision a world where practitioners can sustain themselves through their work, where creative and healing labor is valued fairly, where the "starving artist" trope is replaced by thriving communities.

We envision a world where borders dissolve and practitioners move freely across the planet, creating, healing, collaborating, and inspiring wherever their journey takes them.

This is not a distant dream. We are building this world now, one connection at a time, one artwork at a time, one healing at a time, one practitioner at a time.
